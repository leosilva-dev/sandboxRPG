Tiny Swords asset pack, by pixelfrog-assets (itch.io).

Two generations of the pack are kept side by side because they don't fully
overlap in content or art style:

- free-pack/    -- original free release. 5 recolored armies (Black, Blue,
                   Purple, Red, Yellow), each with Archer/Lancer/Monk/Pawn/
                   Warrior units and Archery/Barracks/Castle/House1-3/
                   Monastery/Tower buildings. Flat single-tone terrain
                   tileset (5 color variants), resources (gold/meat/tools/
                   wood), decorations (bushes/clouds/rocks/duck), particle
                   FX, and a full UI kit (buttons/bars/banners/icons/avatars).

- update-010/   -- newer release, reorganized around two factions (Knights
                   vs Goblins) instead of 5 recolored armies. Knights keep
                   color variants (Blue/Purple/Red/Yellow) for buildings and
                   troops (Archer/Pawn/Warrior + a Dead sprite); Goblins are
                   siege/utility units (Barrel, TNT, Torch), not humanoid
                   troops, in this free tier. Richer terrain (flat + elevation
                   tilemap, single water style, bridges), resources (Gold
                   Mine, Trees, Sheep, generic resource pickups), and a much
                   more polished UI kit (9-slice buttons/banners, cursor
                   pointers, connectable ribbons).

No license/README file was bundled with either download. Before shipping or
monetizing anything built with these, verify current usage terms on the
itch.io page (https://pixelfrog-assets.itch.io/tiny-swords) rather than
assuming — free asset packs on itch.io commonly allow personal/commercial
use in a finished game but forbid reselling/redistributing the raw assets.

Folder/file names were slugified (lowercase, dashes) from the original
"Some Name.png" / "Some Folder" convention for URL-safety. `.aseprite`
source files are NOT in this folder (they're never fetched by the browser,
so shipping them here would bloat the built site) -- they live in
/art-source/tiny-swords/ at the repo root, mirroring this same structure,
for anyone who wants to re-edit the originals in Aseprite later.
